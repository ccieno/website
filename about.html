what's this for?

After Cisco announced changes to the certification programs in 2019, I decided to make the push for CCNP and subsequently the new CCIE Enterprise Core certifications. Specifics can be found here

Whilst much of the content is familiar to many CCNA level engineers (OSPF, VTP, Spanning Tree), there's also a huge amount of new information to learn (LISP, DNA Center, Python, JSON). Much of this is so new to the community that not only is there little written about it so far, but also hands on access and lab time is difficult to come by.

Initially I'll be targetting the ENCOR 350-401 written exam, which is a required exam for both CCNP and CCIE Enterprise certifications. The CCNP requires a further specialisation which I havent yet chosen, whilst the CCIE will still require the famous 8 hour lab exam, albeit an updated iteration to match the new syllabus. A full breakdown of topics for the ENCOR is here

As I start the journey in January of 2020, I hope not only to document my progress and hopefully success, but also to provide lab set ups and a breakdown of the technologies involved.

 

Resources:

  • Cisco Press ENCOR Office Cert Guide. At the time of writing, this is pretty much the only official resource. Whether it's sufficient remains to be seen - previous versions tend to lay a decent foundation but still require further reading
  • CBT Nuggets. I'll be using the Devnet course and once the ENCOR is released in March I'll use that too
  • Udemy. David Bomal has a Python for Network Engineers course available which I'll be using to get a handle on Python due to my total lack of coding experience
  • Cisco Devnet. There looks to be a reasonable amount of new and free content available to give me a decent insight into coding, APIs, and containers etc etc
  • GNS3. Old Faithful is what I'll be using when designing and creating my own labs to begin with. How far it gets me remains to be seen
  • Cisco VIRL. It looks like GNS3 wont be able to provide access to everything I'll need, so I will have to get my hand in my pocket for $200 for 12 months access at some point
  • TBC. Whatever else comes may way will be added to this list
